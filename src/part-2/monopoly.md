A monopoly exists when a single firm is the sole seller of a product with no close substitutes. Unlike a competitive firm, a monopolist faces the entire market demand curve and therefore has pricing power.

## Profit Maximization

A monopolist sets **MR = MC** to maximize profit, then reads the price off the demand curve. Because MR < P for a downward-sloping demand curve, the monopolist sets P > MC.

## Deadweight Loss

The gap between price and marginal cost creates a **deadweight loss** — a welfare cost to society. Output is below the socially optimal level; gains from trades that could have occurred do not.

## Sources of Monopoly Power

- **Barriers to entry** — patents, licenses, high fixed costs
- **Control of key inputs**
- **Natural monopoly** — declining average costs over the relevant range of demand
- **Network effects**

## Policy Responses

- **Antitrust enforcement** — breaking up monopolies or preventing mergers
- **Price regulation** — setting P = MC or P = ATC for natural monopolies
- **Public ownership**
