The supply and demand model is the cornerstone of microeconomic analysis. It describes how prices emerge from the interaction of buyers and sellers in a competitive market.

## The Demand Curve

The demand curve shows the relationship between price and quantity demanded, holding all else constant. It slopes downward: as price rises, consumers buy less.

**Key shifters of demand:**
- Income (normal vs. inferior goods)
- Prices of related goods (substitutes and complements)
- Consumer expectations
- Tastes and preferences

## The Supply Curve

The supply curve shows the relationship between price and quantity supplied. It slopes upward: higher prices make production more profitable, inducing firms to supply more.

**Key shifters of supply:**
- Input costs (labor, capital, raw materials)
- Technology
- Number of sellers
- Government taxes or subsidies

## Equilibrium

Markets clear where supply equals demand. At the equilibrium price, the quantity consumers wish to buy exactly equals the quantity producers wish to sell.
