Elasticity measures how sensitive quantity demanded or supplied is to changes in price or other variables. It is one of the most practically useful concepts in applied economics.

## Price Elasticity of Demand

$$E_d = \frac{\% \Delta Q_d}{\% \Delta P}$$

| Elasticity | Meaning |
|---|---|
| \|E\| > 1 | Elastic — quantity is responsive to price |
| \|E\| = 1 | Unit elastic |
| \|E\| < 1 | Inelastic — quantity is not very responsive |

## Determinants of Elasticity

- **Availability of substitutes** — more substitutes = more elastic
- **Necessity vs. luxury** — necessities tend to be inelastic
- **Time horizon** — demand is more elastic in the long run
- **Share of budget** — larger budget shares tend to be more elastic

## Policy Relevance

Elasticity is critical for tax policy. When demand is **inelastic**, the tax burden falls primarily on consumers. When demand is **elastic**, producers bear more of the burden.
